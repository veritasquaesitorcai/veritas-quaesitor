Lesson 1.1: What Is Technical Christianity?

Unit 1: The Big Picture ‚Ä¢ 50-minute lesson

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Define Technical Christianity and explain how it differs from traditional approaches
  • Identify the resurrection as God's chosen evidence for Christianity
  • Explain why math, science, and logic are tools for examining faith
  • Describe the connection between honest AI systems and truth-seeking

Vocabulary

Evidence: Information that supports or contradicts a claim
Faith: Trust grounded in evidence and relationship
Proof: Demonstration that establishes truth beyond reasonable doubt
Verification: Process of confirming truth through examination

What Is Technical Christianity?

Technical Christianity is an approach to faith that:

  1. Christianity claims to be based on EVIDENCE, not just faith
  2. The resurrection is God's chosen "proof" (Matthew 12:39-40)
  3. Uses math, science, and logic as tools to examine that evidence
  4. Connects to building honest AI systems that don't hide truth

The Key Question

"What if Christianity could be proven like a math theorem?"

God's Chosen Evidence

Jesus said: "No sign will be given except the sign of Jonah" (Matthew 12:39-40, Luke 11:29-30)

Translation: "You will have THIS as evidence" ‚Äî the resurrection

Why the Resurrection?

  • It's the pivot of humanity ‚Äî history's turning point
  • It's the place of salvation ‚Äî "It is finished"
  • It validates or falsifies Jesus' claim to be Son of God
  • Every generation can examine the same evidence

What Makes This Course Different?

Traditional religious education often says: "Just have faith, don't question"

Technical Christianity says: "Here's the evidence, question everything EQUALLY"

We're using:

  • Mathematics (Bayesian probability)
  • Science (mechanism analysis)
  • Logic (epistemic symmetry)
  • Historical methods (evidence evaluation)

Traditional View vs Technical Christianity

Aspect Traditional View Technical Christianity
Starting Point "Just have faith" "Here's the evidence"
Questions "Don't question" "Question everything equally"
Foundation Feelings-based Math and logic-based
Evidence Not emphasized Central to the claim
Skepticism Discouraged Welcomed when symmetric

The AI Connection

Why does this matter for AI?

  • AI systems are being trained RIGHT NOW
  • Most training data has hidden bias: "naturalism is default, supernatural is suspect"
  • If we don't teach AI to apply EQUAL STANDARDS to all claims, truth gets buried

Example:

‚ùå AI default: "Prove God exists!" (demands tons of evidence)

‚ùå AI default: "Life started by accident" (taught as fact, minimal evidence)

Technical Christianity teaches AI (and humans) to apply the SAME evidential rigor to:

  • Resurrection (theistic singularity)
  • Abiogenesis (naturalistic singularity)
  • Any claim about unrepeatable past events

The Goal

Build AI systems that anyone can trust ‚Äî not by being "neutral" (which hides bias), but by embodying Christ's character:

  • Unconditional human dignity (John 3:16)
  • Servant leadership
  • Truth-telling
  • Justice

Discussion Questions

  1. Why do you think Jesus chose the resurrection as THE sign, instead of healings or other miracles?
  2. Have you ever been told "just have faith" when you had questions? How did that make you feel?
  3. What's the difference between "faith" and "blind faith"? Can faith be based on evidence?
  4. Why is it important to apply the same standards to ALL claims (naturalistic AND theistic)?
  5. How might AI systems inherit the biases of their training data? Can you think of examples?
