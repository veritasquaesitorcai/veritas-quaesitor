The Math of the Resurrection

Mathematical Foundations

This framework utilizes rigorous standards of proof used in science and information theory. By updating our priors with evidence, we reach calculated historical confidence levels.

1. The Bayesian Core

\[ P(R|E) = \frac{P(E|R) \times P(R)}{P(E|R) \times P(R) + P(E|\neg R) \times P(\neg R)} \]

We quantify confidence using Bayes' Theorem to update "Prior Probability" (P(R)) to a "Posterior Probability" based on historical evidence (E).

2. The Mechanism Penalty

\[ P(m|H) \propto e^{-\lambda k} \]

Quantifying hypothesis complexity where \( k \) is the count of independent causal mechanisms. This provides a mathematical parsimony filter against ad-hoc naturalistic explanations.

3. The Institutional Proxy Audit

\[ P(H) \to P(H_{Global}) \text{ where } P(H_{Global}) \gg P(H_{Institutional}) \]

Anti-Authority Firewall: Exposing demographic outliers. We recalibrate Priors by identifying where wealth-aligned institutional data contradicts Global Human Majority evidence.

\[ H_{best} = \arg\max_{H_i} \left[ \frac{\text{Explanatory Power}(H_i)}{\text{Ad-hoc Modifications}(H_i)} \right] \]

The hypothesis requiring the fewest independent causal mechanisms while explaining the most evidence categories wins, regardless of prior probability assignments.
