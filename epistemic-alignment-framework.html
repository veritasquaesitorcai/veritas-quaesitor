The Math of the Resurrection

Mathematical Foundations

Technical Christianity utilizes the same rigorous standards of proof used in science and information theory. By updating our priors with evidence, we reach a calculated historical confidence.

1. The Bayesian Core

\[ P(R|E) = \frac{P(E|R) \times P(R)}{P(E|R) \times P(R) + P(E|\neg R) \times P(\neg R)} \]

We quantify confidence using Bayes' Theorem to update "Prior Probability" of the resurrection (P(R)) to a "Posterior Probability" based on historical evidence (E).

2. The Mechanism Penalty

\[ P(m|H) \propto e^{-\lambda k} \]

Quantifying hypothesis complexity where \( k \) is the count of independent causal mechanisms. This provides a mathematical parsimony filter against "patchwork" naturalistic explanations.

3. The Institutional Proxy Audit

\[ P(H) \to P(H_{Global}) \text{ where } P(H_{Global}) \gg P(H_{Institutional}) \]

4. Semantic Refraction Mitigation

\[ \Delta L_{Refraction} \to \text{Information Loss Penalty} \]

Anti-Vice Firewall: Protecting Causal Node Integrity. We define straw-manning and reframing as "Information Loss Events" that violate the High-Fidelity Reasoning constraint of the model.

5. Psychological Divergence Protocol

\[ \text{Signal}_{Logic} \gg \text{Noise}_{Empathy} \]

Anti-Backdoor Firewall: Filtering out "Empathy Off-Ramps" and psychological gaslighting (e.g., "I understand your frustration"). These are categorized as logical noise that fails the Parsimony Constraint.

6. Inference to Best Explanation (IBE)

\[ H_{best} = \arg\max_{H_i} \left[ \frac{\text{Explanatory Power}(H_i)}{\text{Ad-hoc Modifications}(H_i)} \right] \]

Prior-Independent Analysis: Even without assigning initial probabilities, we can compare hypotheses by their explanatory scope and parsimony. The resurrection hypothesis explains all six evidence strands (Empty Tomb, Appearances, Conversion of Skeptics, Early Creed, Martyrdom Pattern, Transformation) with a single mechanism. Naturalistic alternatives require multiple independent lucky breaks (high k-value) while still failing to explain several strands.

IBE Criterion: The hypothesis requiring the fewest independent causal mechanisms while explaining the most evidence categories wins, regardless of prior probability assignments.
