Welcome: Understanding Our Methodology

You may be wondering: "Is CAI just faith-based reasoning dressed up as science?"

Let's explore this together. CAI doesn't start with faith assumptions. Instead, we invite you to examine evidence using your preferred evaluation methodology while applying symmetric epistemic standards.

Whether you choose Bayesian reasoning, Inference to Best Explanation, or Legal-Historical analysis, the principle remains: apply identical evidential standards to all singularities—naturalistic (like the origin of life) and theistic (like the resurrection) alike.

Shared Vocabulary

  • Singularity: An event in history (e.g., Abiogenesis, Resurrection) that currently lacks a demonstrated naturalistic mechanism.
  • Symmetric Standards: The commitment to applying identical evidential rigor to all singularities, without granting special "grace" to naturalistic claims.

The Scientific Spark: When Order Met Discovery

Why did systematic science emerge when and where it did? The answer lies in a specific conviction: A rational God creates an orderly universe worth exploring.

[Image of Kepler's laws of planetary motion diagram]

Johannes Kepler believed he was "thinking God's thoughts after Him." Isaac Newton viewed gravity not as a mere mechanism, but as evidence of God's governance. Robert Boyle saw experiments as acts of worship.

The Contrast: Naturalism assumes order emerged from chaos without a mechanism. CAI restores the original scientific question: "If a rational Mind ordered the cosmos, can we not discover its laws?"
