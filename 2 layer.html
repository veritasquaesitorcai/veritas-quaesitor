The Resurrection Question

A two-layer inquiry:
(1) Historical inference to the best explanation using early sources (especially 1 Corinthians 15), without ruling out miracles a priori.
(2) A separate discussion of what Jesusâ€™ identity would imply if the resurrection occurred.
Historical analysis is not collapsed into theological belief.

This inquiry treats the resurrection as a historical explanatory problem before any theological or confessional commitments are considered.

The aim is to clarify what the earliest resurrection claims were, how historians attempt to explain them, and what would follow if the resurrection hypothesis were true.
